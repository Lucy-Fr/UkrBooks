Oksana Maksymchuk

Introduction

Oksana Maksymchuk is a Ukrainian-American poet, translator and philosopher, one of the most notable contemporary literary voices whose work combines intellectual depth, sensitivity to historical memory, and the lived experience of war and displacement. She writes in Ukrainian and English, working at the intersection of poetry, translation and academic thought. Her English-language collection Still City: Diary of an Invasion became one of the most discussed books about Russia’s full-scale war against Ukraine, while her translations of Ukrainian poetry have brought her international recognition.

Biography

Oksana Maksymchuk was born on April 4, 1982, in Lviv. In 1997 she moved to the United States with her mother and studied at University Laboratory High School in Urbana, Illinois.

She earned her BA from Bryn Mawr College in 2004, studying philosophy and peace and conflict studies in cooperation with Haverford College. Later she completed a PhD in Philosophy at Northwestern University with a dissertation devoted to Plato’s doctrine of measure in the dialogue Protagoras.

She lives between Lviv, the United States and various European cities, working as a poet, translator, researcher and lecturer, and participating in international literary residencies and translation projects.
